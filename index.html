Welcome

College of Arts and Sciences (CAS) as a strong and vibrant service and degree-offering college, the first among equals, in a premier university. Contributory to the Mission of the Nueva Vizcaya State University and in line with its vision of a strong and vibrant college, the College of Arts and Sciences shall provide general education courses to all students from the other colleges of the University, and to serve the special needs of other colleges for major courses in the disciplines of the Arts and Sciences. It shall also offer relevant degree courses that stand to benefit the community in particular and the global community in general.The College of Arts and Sciences is geared towards the preparation of students for responsible, creative and useful membership and leadership in the society through innovative designed to stimulate and develop creative, analytical and critical thinking, effective communication and sound judgment, without sacrificing aesthetic, ethical and cultural values.

NVSU to Get AACCUP's nod for BS Computer Science Program

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) program under the College of Arts and Sciences of the Nueva Vizcaya State University is currently undergoing Level IV Phase II Revisit accreditation by the Accrediting Agency of Chartered Colleges and Universities in the Philippines (AACCUP) from May 29–31 via Zoom platform. ...

The rigorous assessment covers all key areas of the BS Computer Science program, including research, graduate performance, international linkages, community service, and planning processes. The BSCS accreditation task force attended the opening program before engaging in discussions with the accreditors at Room ITE202. The accreditor provided a checklist that must be complied with on the second day of the accreditation process, followed by interviews with the area chairs assigned to each surveyed area.

Faculty Researchers of NVSU CAS Showcase Studies at Research Congress

Faculty researchers from Nueva Vizcaya State University's (NVSU) College of Arts and Sciences (CAS) recently showcased their research studies at the Faculty Research Congress organized by the Philippine Consortium for Science, Mathematics and Technology (PCSMT), Inc. on April 19-21, 2023, at the Ilocos Sur Polytechnic State College-Main Campus in Candon City, Ilocos Sur. ...

NVSU CAS was represented by four faculty researchers who specialize in Mathematics and General Science: Jay Roy D. Velasco, Jr., Jeffrey T. Mendoza, Joseph E. Valdez, and Ruchi Briam James S. Lagitnay. The studies presented by NVSU CAS mentors covered a broad range of topics, including the predictors associated with COVID-19 in Nueva Vizcaya and the effectiveness of a problem-based learning approach in enhancing problem-solving skills in chemistry for high school students. The congress this year is themed "Advancing the Frontiers of Math, Science, and Technology through Interdisciplinary Research." Its goal is to showcase the research capabilities of different academic institutions in the country and encourage collaborative research among them. The attendees, consisting of researchers and faculty members, also emphasized the importance of research in promoting technology and innovation in the country.
